**Human-in-the-loop** (**HITL**) in Phrony means a person can **pause** an agent **[run](/product/concepts/run)** before certain **integration operations** execute—typically after the model has chosen to call an operation that requires **human approval**. The run waits until someone **approves** or **rejects** (or otherwise resolves) the pending step in **[Approvals](/product/dashboard/approvals)** or the **Run** inspector.

## Requirements

For approval gates on **integration operations** to **pause** the run (instead of failing or skipping):

1. The **[agent](/product/concepts/agent)** must use **HITL** **execution mode** (not **Request**).
2. The **operation** (HTTP or built-in) must have **Require approval for this operation** turned on under **Integrations**, or the HTTP integration may use **Always require approval** as a blanket default—see **[Gating operations](/product/hitl/gating-operations)**.

**Request** mode is for runs that should **not** wait on humans for those gates; approval-required operations behave differently there (they are not executed as pausing HITL steps).

## What HITL is not

- **Not** the same as **Sequential / Parallel** on **[Agent version](/product/concepts/agent-version)** → **Multi-agent**—those only affect **sub-agent** delegation batching. **[Agent](/product/concepts/agent)** → Cautions.
- **Not** the same as **AITL** (**[Agent in the loop](/product/agent-in-the-loop/overview)**)—parent/Sub-agent **coordination** when a **child** asks the **parent** (or human) a question mid-run. When a **human** answers that question (HITL session root), see **[Human escalation](/product/agent-in-the-loop/human-escalation)**—distinct from **operation** approval gates above.
- **Not** a replacement for **[Guard & anomaly](/product/dashboard/guard-anomaly)** or **Anomaly control (L1)** on a version—those are separate review and policy paths; they can coexist with HITL.

## Who does what

| Role | Typical HITL role |
| --- | --- |
| **Owner**, **Admin**, **Developer** | Set **HITL** on the agent, configure **operations** and versions. |
| **Operator** | Often the person who resolves items in **Approvals** or in-run review UI. |

See **[Limits, permissions, and plans](/product/reference/platform-rules)** for dashboard roles.
