title: "Gating operations"
description: "Require approval per operation, HTTP integration defaults, and how gating interacts with HITL."
---

Pauses for humans are tied to **integration operations**—the concrete actions defined under **Integrations** and enabled on an **[agent version](/product/concepts/agent-version)** → **Operations**.

## Require approval for this operation

On each **operation** (HTTP or built-in), the dashboard offers **Require approval for this operation** (wording may match your build).

- **On** — when the model selects this **operation**, the run can **pause** until someone approves in **[Approvals](/product/dashboard/approvals)** or **Run** detail—**if** the **[agent](/product/concepts/agent)** is in **HITL** **execution mode**.
- **Off** — the operation runs without that human gate (subject to **[Guard](/product/dashboard/guard-anomaly)** and other limits).

Prefer **per-operation** gating: keep read-only or low-risk **operations** ungated; turn on approval only for sends, payments, deletes, or other high-impact calls.

Full connector context: **[Integration](/product/concepts/integration)** → Human approval (HITL).

## HTTP: Always require approval

On **HTTP** integrations, **Always require approval** applies a **default** gate to **every** **operation** on that connector unless an **operation** overrides it. Use sparingly when **all** outbound calls from that integration should be reviewed.

## Built-in integrations

Built-in **operations** follow the same pattern: enable or disable **Require approval for this operation** on each **operation** as your risk model requires.

## Sub-agents and parents

A **HITL** **parent** may pause on its own integration **operations**. A **Sub-agent** **child** run may also pause if the child’s **operations** require approval and the child agent is **HITL**—see **[Multi-agent](/product/multi-agent/parent-and-sub-agent)**.
