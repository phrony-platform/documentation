**Agent-in-the-loop** (**AITL**) is Phrony’s pattern for **parent ↔ Sub-agent** coordination: when a **child** run needs clarification or data only the **parent** (or, in some setups, a **human**) can supply, the runtime **pauses** the child, surfaces a structured **question** to the parent model, and requires a **resolution** before work continues.

## Prerequisites

AITL only applies when **[multi-agent delegation](/product/multi-agent/overview)** is in use: a **Request** or **HITL** **parent** and at least one **Sub-agent** child on the allow-list. There is no separate “AITL toggle”—behavior is part of the **sub-agent** execution path.

## AITL vs HITL (human-in-the-loop)

| Concept | Who acts | Typical trigger |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **HITL** | **Human** approves or rejects **[integration](/product/concepts/integration)** **operations** before they run. | **Require approval for this operation**, **HITL** **execution mode** on the agent. See **[Human in the loop](/product/hitl/overview)**. |
| **AITL** | **Parent agent** (and optionally **human** if the session root is **HITL**) answers a **child Sub-agent** that paused with a question. | Sub-agent needs missing context; runtime delivers a **pending** question to the parent. |

A single **session** can use **both**: for example a **HITL** parent may hit **Approvals** for a gated HTTP **operation** while also resolving an **AITL** question from a child.

## Who sees what

- **Parent** **[run](/product/concepts/run)** trace shows when a child is **waiting for parent** (or human escalation) and the steps to **respond**.
- **Child** run may show **Waiting for parent** / similar until the parent resolves the request.
- **[Approvals](/product/dashboard/approvals)** may list **AITL escalations** when the parent escalates to a **human** (only when the **session root** is **HITL**).
