title: "Guard L1 in production"
description: "Step-by-step: anomaly control on a version, rules, testing, deploy, and review workflows."
---

**Guard** (anomaly) **L1** is per-**[agent version](/product/concepts/agent-version)**: you turn on **Anomaly control (L1)** under **Limits & Safety** and define **rules** on tool calls (for example require review, block, or allow). The goal is to keep **production** agents from taking destructive or out-of-policy **actions** through **[integrations](/product/concepts/integration)** without oversight.

This guide is about **reducing risk**, not replacing your own change-management process. For workspace-wide anomaly **proposals** and **incidents**, see **[Guard & anomaly](/product/dashboard/guard-anomaly)** and **[Approvals](/product/dashboard/approvals)**.

**Prerequisites**

- **Guard** is enabled for your workspace (sidebar item may show **Beta**; otherwise billing or support may need to turn the feature on).
- You can edit **Version configuration** and resolve **[Approvals](/product/dashboard/approvals)** if you use **review** rules.

---

## Step 1 — Start on a testing snapshot

1. Open the **[agent](/product/concepts/agent)** → **Version configuration**.
2. Select a **testing** snapshot (**live** versions are read-only in the UI).

Never enable risky rules for the first time directly on **live** without a prior test pass.

---

## Step 2 — Turn on L1 and add rules

1. Open **Limits & Safety**.
2. Enable **Anomaly control (L1)**.
3. Use the **Rules** builder to scope tool calls: which operations or patterns require **human review**, are **blocked**, or are **allowed**.

Invalid rule sets fail **Save** with builder messages—fix before continuing. Field reference: **[Agent version](/product/concepts/agent-version)** → **Limits & Safety**.

---

## Step 3 — Test with Test run

1. **Save** the testing snapshot.
2. Run **Test run** with scenarios that should **hit**, **miss**, or **block** each rule.
3. Confirm pauses, denials, or **[run](/product/concepts/run)** outcomes match your intent in the inspector.

**Test run** uses the **selected** snapshot—ideal for validating L1 before deploy.

---

## Step 4 — Roll out to live carefully

1. When behavior is correct, **Deploy** that version so it becomes **live** for **[triggers](/product/concepts/trigger)** and API traffic.
2. Communicate to operators: **Approvals** or in-run prompts may increase when **review** rules fire.

If the agent is **blocked** at the workspace level pending anomaly review, **no** new runs start until cleared—see **[Limits & plans](/product/reference/platform-rules)**.

---

## Step 5 — Operate and tune

1. Monitor **[sessions](/product/concepts/session)** and **[Approvals](/product/dashboard/approvals)** for backlog or repeated false positives.
2. Adjust rules on a **new testing** snapshot, **Save**, then **Deploy** again—do not expect to edit **live** in place.

Use **[Guard & anomaly](/product/dashboard/guard-anomaly)** for **rule proposals** and **incidents** when your workspace exposes them.

---

## Practices that reduce production damage

- **Least privilege on Operations:** only enable integrations the version truly needs.
- **Block before allow:** default-deny patterns for high-risk tools, then explicitly allow safe paths.
- **Review** sensitive writes (payments, deletes, mass updates) so **[Approvals](/product/dashboard/approvals)** catches them.
- **Separate** experimental versions from **live**; deploy only after **Test run** evidence.

---

## Common pitfalls

| Issue | What to check |
| --- | --- |
| Rules ignored | L1 toggle off; wrong snapshot **live**; operation not covered by rule. |
| Everything blocks | Over-broad rules; refine allow paths. |
| Runs won’t start | **[Guard](/product/dashboard/guard-anomaly)** workspace block or plan overage—**[Limits & plans](/product/reference/platform-rules)**. |
| False sense of safety | L1 does not replace input validation, secrets hygiene, or integration-side auth. |
